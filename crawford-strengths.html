Character
Strengths

Everyone has character strengths. Not skills you've learned, but qualities that are already part of how you show up. This exercise is based on the VIA Classification of Character Strengths, a research-backed framework that identifies 24 strengths grouped under 6 core virtues.

You'll rate 48 short statements about yourself. There are no right or wrong answers. Just be honest about what feels true, not what you wish were true. At the end, you'll see which strengths come most naturally to you.

Signature Strengths

How to read your results

Your signature strengths are the qualities you draw on most naturally. They tend to show up when you're at your best, and leaning into them intentionally can help you navigate challenges, build better relationships, and make decisions that feel more like you. They're worth knowing, and worth using on purpose.

A few things worth keeping in mind. This is a snapshot, not a fixed portrait. Your strengths can shift over time as your circumstances, relationships, and priorities change. If you take this again in six months, some things may look different.

All 24 character strengths carry equal weight. A lower score does not mean a deficit. It simply means that particular quality may not be one you lean on as instinctively right now. That said, your lower scores can be useful too. They can point toward areas where intentional growth might open up new ways of showing up in your life, if that's something you're interested in.
